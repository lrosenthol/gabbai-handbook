# Introduction / מָבוֹא

This handbook is a guide for synagogue officers (*gabbaim*) and those who assist in the conduct of the service.  It covers the liturgical order, the honours (*aliyot*, *hagbahah*, *gelilah*), and the practical responsibilities that fall to those who manage the Torah reading.

## Purpose of This Guide / מַטָּרַת הַמַּדְרִיךְ

The gabbai occupies a central position in the synagogue service.  A well-prepared gabbai ensures that the service flows smoothly, that honours are distributed appropriately, and that errors in the Torah reading are corrected with care and without embarrassment to the reader.

## How to Use This Handbook

Each chapter addresses a distinct area of responsibility.  Cross-references are provided where topics overlap.  Hebrew terms are introduced alongside their English equivalents, since the practitioner will encounter both in synagogue use.

> A gabbai who knows the *halacha* and the custom of the congregation can fulfill his role with confidence.
